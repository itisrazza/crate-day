Crate Day

Crate Day is an informal national holiday in New Zealand to celebrate the beginning of summer and share a crate of beer with mates. It occurs on the first Saturday of December every year.

It is celebrated by downing a crate of 12 beers hourly from 12 PM to 12 AM on the following Sunday. The following rules are implemented. They are like laws, but more important.

A 12 crate of beers can be substituted with a bottle of sprits. If you're unable to continue, your crate will be forfeited.

Commandments

  1. Thou shall begin at midday.
  2. Thou shall listen to The Rock Cratest Hits.
  3. Thou shall wear thy stubbies and thy singlet.
  4. Thou shall support thy Crate of Origin.
  5. Thou shalt not toss any salad.
  6. Thou shall respect thy neighbour.
  7. Thou only beef that shall attend National Crate Day is thy beef for thy BBQ.
  8. Thou shall look out for thy mates, even Dave.
  9. Don't let the sun get thy best of you and your provisions.
  10. Thou shall always keep rules 1-9 top of mind, to ensure we continue The Rock National Crate Day legacy for centuries to come.
